Today's Highlights

Ramping up mitochondrial DNA replication

This study addresses how mitochondrial DNA (mtDNA) replication is regulated to meet cellular energy demands. The authors reveal new mechanisms that enhance mtDNA replication, providing insights into mitochondrial biogenesis and potential therapeutic targets for diseases linked to mitochondrial dysfunction.

Targeting RNA structure to treat prostate cancer

The paper investigates the potential of targeting specific RNA structures as a novel strategy to combat prostate cancer. The findings demonstrate that modulating RNA conformation can disrupt oncogenic pathways, offering a promising avenue for developing RNA-based therapeutics in cancer treatment.

Lasso peptide targets drug-resistant bacteria

This research focuses on discovering new antimicrobial agents to fight drug-resistant bacteria. The authors identify a lasso peptide that selectively targets resistant bacterial strains, highlighting its potential as a novel antibiotic scaffold to address the growing threat of antibiotic resistance.

TIM-3 regulates microglial function

The study explores the role of the immune checkpoint protein TIM-3 in regulating microglial cells in the brain. Results show that TIM-3 modulates microglial activity, influencing neuroinflammation and suggesting new therapeutic targets for neurodegenerative diseases.
