Today's Highlights

Image-guided surgical approach using CJ215 dye for cancer detection

This study addresses the challenge of accurately detecting and removing cancerous tissues during surgery. The authors developed a novel image-guided surgical technique employing the commercially available dye CJ215, which enhances real-time visualization of tumors and improves preclinical imaging of cancer progression in deep tissues, potentially increasing surgical precision and patient outcomes.

Mechanistic insights and applications of targeted protein degradation in cancer therapy

Focusing on targeted protein degradation (TPD) as a therapeutic strategy in cancer, this Review analyzes the molecular mechanisms underlying TPD and evaluates the properties of current and emerging degrader compounds. The work highlights the advantages of TPD modalities, offering a comprehensive perspective on their potential to revolutionize cancer treatment by selectively eliminating disease-relevant proteins.
