Today's Highlights

Ramping up mitochondrial DNA replication

This study addresses how cells regulate mitochondrial DNA replication to meet energetic demands. The authors identify key molecular mechanisms that enhance replication rates, revealing new insights into mitochondrial biogenesis and its potential role in metabolic diseases.

Targeting RNA structure to treat prostate cancer

The paper investigates the potential of manipulating RNA structures as a therapeutic strategy for prostate cancer. The findings demonstrate that targeting specific RNA conformations can disrupt cancer cell growth, highlighting a novel avenue for precision oncology interventions.

Image-guided surgical approach using CJ215 dye for cancer detection

Ali Sonay presents an innovative surgical technique employing the commercially available dye CJ215 to enhance real-time visualization and precise excision of cancerous tissues. This method improves preclinical imaging of tumor progression in deep tissues, offering significant advancements for surgical oncology.

Mechanistic insights and applications of targeted protein degradation in cancer therapy

Hinterndorfer et al. review the underlying mechanisms of targeted protein degradation (TPD) and evaluate the current and emerging classes of degrader compounds. Author Correction: Parvalbumin-expressing basket-cell network plasticity induced by experience regulates adult learning

This correction note addresses amendments to prior findings on how experience-driven plasticity in parvalbumin-expressing basket-cell networks influences adult learning. The update ensures accuracy in the reported mechanisms linking neural circuit adaptability to cognitive function.
