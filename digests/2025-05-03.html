Image-Guided Surgical Approach Using CJ215 Dye for Cancer Detection

This study addresses the challenge of precisely detecting and removing cancerous tissues during surgery. The authors developed a novel image-guided surgical technique leveraging the commercially available dye CJ215, which enhances real-time visualization of tumors and improves preclinical imaging of cancer progression in deep tissues, potentially advancing surgical outcomes and cancer management.

Mechanistic Insights and Therapeutic Applications of Targeted Protein Degradation in Cancer

Focusing on targeted protein degradation (TPD) as a promising cancer treatment strategy, this review elucidates the underlying mechanisms and evaluates various classes of degrader compounds. Hinterndorfer et al. highlight the advantages and expanding applications of TPD, underscoring its potential to transform cancer pharmacotherapy by selectively eliminating disease-associated proteins.
